GRAMMAR Module 1 – Les Temps Verbaux (Tenses)

📌 Vue d'ensemble des temps

Le BTS Cameroun teste principalement ces temps. Voici un tableau complet :

TempsFormeUsageExemple BTS
Present SimpleV / V+s (3e pers)Habitudes, vérités généralesShe works at the hospital.
Present Continuousam/is/are + V-ingAction en cours maintenantHe is studying for his BTS.
Past SimpleV-ed / irrégulierAction terminée dans le passéShe went to school yesterday.
Past Continuouswas/were + V-ingAction en cours dans le passéHe was sleeping when I called.
Present Perfecthave/has + V-ppPassé lié au présentI have done my homework.
Past Perfecthad + V-ppAvant une autre action passéeShe had eaten before he arrived.
Future – willwill + VDécision spontanée, promesseI will help you tomorrow.
Future – going toam/is/are going to + VIntention planifiéeShe is going to study medicine.

🔑 Mots-clés pour identifier le bon temps

IndicateurTemps
yesterday, last week/year, ago, in 2021Past Simple
now, at the moment, currently, look!Present Continuous
already, just, yet, ever, never, since, for, recentlyPresent Perfect
before, by the time, after, when (passé)Past Perfect
always, usually, every day, sometimes, oftenPresent Simple
tomorrow, next week, in the futureFuture (will/going to)
✅ ASTUCE BTS : Dans le fill-in-the-blank, cherche toujours l'indicateur de temps dans la phrase ! Si tu vois "yesterday" → Past Simple. Si tu vois "since" → Present Perfect.

⚠️ Since vs For

MotUsageExemple
sincePoint de départ (date/événement)I have lived here since 2019. / She has been sick since Monday.
forDuréeHe has worked here for 5 years. / I waited for two hours.
❌ Erreurs fréquentes :
✗ I live here since 2020. → ✅ I have lived here since 2020.
✗ She go to school yesterday. → ✅ She went to school yesterday.
✗ He is work now. → ✅ He is working now.

📌 Fiche de résumé – Tenses
À retenir :
• Past Simple = action finie dans le passé (ago, yesterday, last...)
• Present Perfect = passé lié au présent (already, just, yet, since, for, ever, never)
• Past Perfect = avant une autre action passée (had + pp)
• Future will = décision spontanée / Future going to = intention planifiée

GRAMMAR Module 2 – Conditional Sentences

📌 Les 4 types de conditionnels

TypeStructureUsageExemple
Zero (0)If + present, presentVérités universelles / habitudesIf you heat water, it boils.
First (1st)If + present, will + VSituation réelle/possible dans le futurIf she studies, she will pass.
Second (2nd)If + past simple, would + VSituation hypothétique / irréelle présenteIf I had money, I would buy a car.
Third (3rd)If + past perfect, would have + ppRegret / situation irréelle dans le passéIf I had known, I would have eaten.
⚡ ASTUCE BTS : La question classique BTS est : "Had I know I ______ all the food before she arrived."
→ "Had I known" = inversion de "If I had known" = 3rd conditional → Réponse : would have eaten
❌ Erreurs fréquentes :
✗ If she will come, I will be happy. → ✅ If she comes, I will be happy. (1st cond.)
✗ If I would have money, I would help. → ✅ If I had money, I would help. (2nd cond.)

GRAMMAR Module 3 – Reported Speech (Discours Rapporté)

📌 Règles fondamentales

Le reported speech (indirect speech) sert à rapporter ce que quelqu'un a dit. Il faut changer :

  • Le verbe introducteur (said, told, asked, wondered...)
  • Les temps verbaux (backshift)
  • Les pronoms
  • Les expressions de temps

🔄 Tableau de Backshift des temps

Direct SpeechReported Speech
Present Simple → "I work"Past Simple → He said he worked
Present Continuous → "I am working"Past Continuous → She said she was working
Past Simple → "I went"Past Perfect → He said he had gone
Present Perfect → "I have done"Past Perfect → She said she had done
will → "I will come"would → He said he would come
can → "I can help"could → She said she could help

🗣️ Changements de pronoms et d'expressions

DirectReported
nowthen / at that time
todaythat day
yesterdaythe day before / the previous day
tomorrowthe next day / the following day
herethere
thisthat
I / me / myhe/she / him/her / his/her
📌 Questions en Reported Speech :
Direct: "Where did I leave my phone?" Peter asked.
Reported: Peter asked where he had left his phone. ← (BTS 2021!)

Direct: "Are you ready?" She asked.
Reported: She asked if/whether I was ready.

GRAMMAR Module 4 – Passive Voice (Voix Passive)

📌 Structure de base

Formule : Subject + to be (conjugué) + Past Participle (+ by + agent)

Active: The teacher corrects the papers.
Passive: The papers are corrected by the teacher.

🔄 Voix passive par temps

TempsPassifExemple
Present Simpleam/is/are + ppRice is grown in Cameroon.
Past Simplewas/were + ppThe letter was written yesterday.
Present Perfecthas/have been + ppThe book has been published.
Futurewill be + ppThe exam will be held in June.
Modalmodal + be + ppThis must be done now.

GRAMMAR Module 7 – Question Forms

📌 Question Tags (très fréquent au BTS)

Règle : Si la phrase est positive → tag négatif. Si la phrase est négative → tag positif.

She works here, doesn't she?
They aren't coming, are they?
You have done it, haven't you?
He can swim, can't he?

📌 Indirect Questions

Règle : En indirect question, on utilise l'ordre normal (S+V), pas d'inversion !

Direct: Where does he live?
Indirect: I wonder where he lives. (pas d'inversion, pas d'auxiliaire do/does)

Direct: Did she pass the exam?
Indirect: I want to know if/whether she passed the exam.

READING Section B – Reading Comprehension

📌 Méthode BTS en 5 étapes

  1. Lire les questions AVANT le texte – Pour savoir quoi chercher.
  2. Skimming (survol) – Lire rapidement pour comprendre l'idée générale.
  3. Scanning (repérage) – Chercher les informations précises (noms, chiffres, dates).
  4. Répondre en phrase complète – Jamais une réponse d'un seul mot.
  5. Reformuler – Ne pas copier mot pour mot ; utilise tes propres mots.
✅ Formules types pour répondre :
• "According to the text, ..." → According to the text, insects spread diseases through mechanical transmission.
• "The text states that..." → The text states that mosquitoes are the greatest menace.
• Pour donner une raison : "...because / due to the fact that / since..."

📄 Texte BTS 2023 – HOW INSECTS MAKE US SICK

People commonly use the term "Insect" to include not true insects—Six legged creatures such as flies, flacs, mosquitoes, lice and beetles—but also eight legged creatures such as mites and ticks. The vast majority of insects are harmless to man and some are very beneficial. Without them, many of the plants and trees that people and animals depend on for food would not be pollinated or bear fruits. Of course there are insects that annoy man and beast with their painful bite or simply by their presence in vast number. Some also wreak havoc on crops. Worse however is the fact that insects spread sickness and death. Insects borne diseases presently, every one out of six people are infected with a disease acquired through insects.


There are two main ways that insects serve as vectors-transmiters of disease. The first is by mechanical transmission. Just as people can rack dirt into a home on unclean shoes, houseflies may carry on their feet millions of micro-organisms that in large enough doses can cause disease. Flies can pick up contamination from faeces for example and pass it on when they land on our food or drink causing illnesses such as typhoid, dysentery and even cholera.


When insects harbor viruses, bacteria or parasites inside their bodies, they can spread diseases a second way—by passing it on to us through a bite. Only a small percentage of insects transmit disease to humans in this way. For instance, although there are thousands of species of mosquitoes, only the anopheles transmits malaria—the world's second deadliest communicable disease (after tuberculosis).

✏️ Answer the questions (Style BTS 2023)

✅ CORRECTION DÉTAILLÉE :

Q1. Diseases caused by insects are called insect borne diseases.

Q2. One important role insects play is pollination: without them, many plants and trees would not be pollinated or bear fruits.

Q3. a) The first way is by mechanical transmission: insects carry micro-organisms on their feet and pass them to our food.
b) The second way is through a bite: when insects harbor viruses or parasites, they spread them by biting humans.

💡 Conseil : Note that each answer uses a complete sentence and reformulates the text in different words — never copy-paste!

✏️ Practice – Write your essay (15 lines minimum)

Subject: Would you like to leave your country after your studies and go to work in a developed country? Why or why not? Give reasons for your choice. (BTS 2023)

✅ GUIDE DE CORRECTION :

Introduction (2-3 lines) : Introduce the topic. E.g.: "Nowadays, many young people dream of working abroad after their studies. This is a controversial topic with both advantages and disadvantages."

Body – Arguments FOR (3-4 lines) : "First of all, developed countries offer better career opportunities and higher salaries. Moreover, one can acquire new skills and experience..."

Body – Arguments AGAINST (3-4 lines) : "However, leaving one's country means abandoning one's family and culture. Furthermore, brain drain is harmful to developing countries like Cameroon..."

Conclusion (2-3 lines) : "In conclusion, the decision to emigrate depends on one's personal goals. In my opinion, one should work abroad to gain experience and then return to serve one's country."

Critères de notation (10 marks) : Content (4) + Organisation (3) + Language (3)
